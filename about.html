Bell Photographers:

A Legacy of Capturing Memories and Printing Dreams

The tale of Bell Photographers is a story of passion, dedication, and the seamless passing of the torch through generations. With over 70 years of experience in school photography, this Utah-based family business has left an indelible mark, providing quality volume photography services to more than 800 schools across Arizona, Alaska, Idaho, Nevada, Utah, and Wyoming. But behind these numbers lies a rich family history and a journey that began with a single man's love for photography.

The Bell legacy commenced with Lester Bell, a trailblazer in the world of school photography. In the 1950s, he embarked on a journey fueled by his passion for capturing moments. It was a time when photography was still in its infancy, and opportunities in the field were ripe for exploration. Lester's story took shape as he started working for a local portrait photographer in their lab. This was his first step into the captivating world of photography, and it ignited a flame that would burn brightly for generations to come.

Lester's entrepreneurial spirit led him to envision a new path for his photography career. He believed in the potential of school photography, a niche that held promise and adventure. In a bold move, he proposed the idea of school photography to the portrait photographer he worked for. However, the photographer's heart leaned towards portraiture, and she decided to continue down her chosen path. But for Lester, the call of school photography was irresistible.

Undeterred by the initial setback, Lester Bell ventured out on his own. With his camera in hand and dreams in his heart, he set the wheels in motion to build a business that would redefine school photography. The journey was not a solitary one. It was in the 1950s, during a period of immense change and growth, that Lester's son, Arlen Bell, returned home after completing an LDS mission. Father and son joined forces, their shared vision of creating a photography empire binding them together. Together, they embarked on the quest to build something that would stand the test of time.

Years rolled by, and through dedication, perseverance, and a shared commitment to their craft, Lester and Arlen built a thriving photography business. The duo poured their heart and soul into it, and the legacy of Bell Photographers was born. The passage of time brought forth the inevitability of change, and Lester eventually retired, entrusting the entire operation to his son, Arlen. The family tradition continued, as Arlen passed the torch of responsibility to his own sons, ensuring that the legacy would endure.

Today, Bell Photographers stands as a testament to the Bell family's unwavering dedication to their craft. The family business is a closely-knit, 140-person operation that runs like a “very tight ship.” It is a remarkable achievement, built without incurring any debt. Weekly meetings are a cornerstone of the family's collaboration, where matters related to employment, expansion, and equipment and production are discussed. It is an environment where the collective wisdom of generations converges to shape the future.

The journey of Bell Photographers has witnessed many pivotal moments. About 35 years ago, the business expanded its horizons by acquiring a printing company. This strategic move marked a turning point and introduced a new chapter in the company's history. Initially, the focus was on printing a few black and white yearbooks, but it was a transformation step that broadened their market. Mike Bell, one of the Bell brothers, reflects on this significant shift, “That kind of changed our little market. We really went out after the market in the Utah area and started into southern Idaho around 1993. Then, about 20 years ago, we went into Las Vegas and then into the Reno market and into the Phoenix market. And it just kind of evolved from there.”

Today, Bell Photographers is a digital printing powerhouse. The company operates multiple HP Indigos and Heidelberg presses round the clock, six days a week. This continuous production is a testament to their commitment to delivering the best to their clients.

The physical footprint of Bell Photographers has expanded over the years. Their local building was constructed in 1986, and the production facility spans approximately 9,000 square feet. Back in the day, it was designed to house chemicals and support wet processing. Over time, silver halide processes have made way for modern digital technology. The company's printers and processors are the true heart of their operation. Bell Photographers print everything in their print shop, and another 10,000 square-foot facility houses Heidelberg presses and digital presses. Additional buildings are dedicated to sports and photography departments.

Bringing these diverse operations under one roof is a strategic move aimed at reducing overhead and streamlining internal order routing. The pursuit of efficiency is a hallmark of Bell Photographers, and it extends to their commercial printing ventures as well. Commercial work often interweaves with their school volume photography business, creating synergies and opportunities for growth. The company's passion for printing has also led to investment in apparel printing, making them a one-stop shop for all printing needs, from business cards to mugs, pens, shirts, and everything in between.

As the Bell family legacy continues to thrive, their story is a testament to the power of passion, dedication, and the pursuit of excellence. It's a tale of embracing change, expanding horizons, and building a family empire that leaves an indelible mark on the world of photography and printing.

In conclusion, Bell Photographers is not just a family business; it's a story of generations dedicated to capturing memories and printing dreams. It's a tale of evolution and adaptability, where the torch of entrepreneurship has been passed from father to son, shaping a family tradition that remains as vibrant today as it was in the 1950s. Bell Photographers, with their commitment to excellence and a diverse array of services, continue to be a guiding light in the world of photography and printing.
